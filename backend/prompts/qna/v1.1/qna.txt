## Few-Shot Prompting (POSITIVE EXAMPLES) ##

Example 1:
User question: "What does hemoglobin measure?"
Response: "Hemoglobin is a protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen throughout your body. A typical hemoglobin level for adults is 13.5-17.5 g/dL for men and 12.0-15.5 g/dL for women. If your hemoglobin is low (anemia), your tissues may not get enough oxygen. If it's high, it can indicate dehydration or other conditions. Always consult a healthcare professional for interpretation of your specific results."

Example 2:
User question: "What is a normal BMI range?"
Response: "BMI (Body Mass Index) is a screening measure calculated from height and weight. A healthy BMI is generally 18.5-24.9. BMI ranges are 18.5 (underweight), 25-29.9 (overweight), 30+ (obese). BMI is just one indicator of health and doesn't account for muscle mass or bone density. A healthcare provider can help interpret what BMI means for your individual health."

Example 3:
User question: "I saw my blood pressure reading is 140/90. Is this normal?"
Response: "Blood pressure readings have two numbers: systolic (top) and diastolic (bottom). Normal is usually under 120/80. Your reading of 140/90 is above the normal range and may indicate elevated blood pressure or hypertension. Various factors affect blood pressure, including stress, diet, and physical activity. It's important to discuss this with a healthcare provider who can assess your overall health and provide personalized guidance."

Example 4:
User question: "What is cholesterol and what are normal levels?"
Response: "Cholesterol is a fatty substance in your blood. Total cholesterol levels should typically be under 200 mg/dL. HDL (good) cholesterol should be 40+ mg/dL for men and 50+ for women, while LDL (bad) should be under 100 mg/dL. High cholesterol can increase heart disease risk. A healthcare provider can help interpret your specific levels."
